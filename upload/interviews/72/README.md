## Interview Context

Cassidy is one of Canada's leading additive manufacturing practitioners with 18 years of mechanical engineering experience and 16 years specifically in AM. As Head of AM Engineering at Exergy Solutions, he leads customer projects across aerospace, industrial equipment, and other sectors. His background includes a PhD in metal additive manufacturing (focus: electric motor applications) and extensive experience with both R&D and production-scale 3D printing systems.

This interview was part of the AddManuChain customer discovery initiative, designed to validate assumptions about AM adoption barriers, supply chain fragmentation, and where digital solutions can add the most value.

---

## Key Takeaways

### Problem 1: Digital Thread is Broken Across the Entire AM Workflow
- **Finding:** Design files, process parameters, machine data, post-processing records, and inspection results are siloed in separate systems with no unified traceability
- **Impact:** Especially critical for regulated industries (aerospace, defense, medical, O&G) where traceability is a compliance requirement
- **Opportunity:** Unified digital thread platform could be AddManuChain's highest-value feature

### Problem 2: AM Adoption Requires Education, Not Just Machines
- **Finding:** Canada has excess machine capacity; the bottleneck is customer knowledge about when and why to use AM
- **Impact:** Companies overapply AM to commodity parts where it adds no value, leading to failed projects
- **Opportunity:** Intelligent part screening and educational content could prevent waste and build customer confidence

### Problem 3: Supply Chain Procurement is Fragmented
- **Finding:** Customers must vet 20-30 different AM service providers to find the right capabilities and cost structure
- **Impact:** Months spent on supplier evaluation; powder sourcing is especially challenging
- **Opportunity:** Transparent marketplace with capability mapping and standardized powder sourcing could save significant time/cost

### Problem 4: AI in AM is Overhyped; Limited Practical Applications Today
- **Finding:** Process parameter optimization, print orientation, and support structure generation offer real value; most other AI applications are still immature
- **Impact:** Don't oversell AI magic; focus on pragmatic, proven features
- **Opportunity:** As industry matures, AI-driven design tools and computational design will become more critical

### Problem 5: Platform Concept is Valid but Needs Extreme Focus
- **Finding:** AddManuChain's ecosystem concept is sound, but **only if it solves a specific, clearly articulated problem** for customers
- **Impact:** Trying to solve everything at once will result in no adoption; narrow target market and iterate
- **Opportunity:** Start with a single vertical (O&G, aerospace, or medical) where the problem is acute and the customer base is sizable

## Insights for Product Direction

### Highest Priority
1. **Digital Thread Integration**
   - Unified tracking from CAD through delivery
   - Powder batch tracking + machine state logging
   - Post-processing integration
   - Compliance documentation generation

2. **Part Evaluation Tool**
   - Automated screening (AM vs. conventional manufacturing)
   - ROI calculator
   - Use-case matching

3. **Supplier Marketplace**
   - Capability transparency
   - Certification pathway templates
   - Cost comparison

### Strategic Insight from Cassidy
**"You really have to nail down. What is the exact problem that you're solving for the client that they can't solve in any other way?"**

This should guide all product decisions. Focus beats scope.
