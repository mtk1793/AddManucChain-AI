**Topic:** Additive Manufacturing for Naval Spares, Victoria-Class Submarine Fleet Resilience, and Sovereign Defence Capacity

## Key Context
Cameron Munro is a Defence Scientist at DRDC Atlantic Research Centre leading a groundbreaking initiative to develop additive manufacturing capacity for critical spare parts on Canada's Victoria-class submarine fleet. The project is funded by NSERC (Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council) and CFI (Canada Foundation for Innovation), with partnerships from industry and Dalhousie University.

The core challenge: Victoria-class submarines were built in the 1980s–1990s, and many original suppliers have exited the market or consolidated. The Canadian Navy faces a supply chain vulnerability where critical components may no longer be available through traditional procurement channels. This project aims to build *domestic industrial capacity* so the Navy can own the R&D and avoid single-supplier lock-in.

### His Vision (Direct Quote)
*"This project isn't just about producing parts. It's about building long-term industrial capacity and allowing the Navy to own the R&D—so they're not locked into one proprietary solution and can competitively source what they need."*
