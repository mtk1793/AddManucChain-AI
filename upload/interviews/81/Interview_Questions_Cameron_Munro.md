# Interview 81: Cameron Munro — Interview Questions
**Defence Scientist, DRDC Atlantic Research Centre (Defence Research and Development Canada)**

---

## 5 Main Questions

### 1. **40-Year Supplier Lock-In: How Does 3D Printing Unlock Vendor Independence for Victoria-Class Subs?**
You're leading an effort to use 3D printing to manufacture critical parts for Canada's Victoria-class submarines. What specific components (hydraulic fittings, seals, brackets, heat exchangers) are you targeting first? What's the current cost and lead-time difference between sourcing from the original supplier vs. printing domestically?

### 2. **Naval Certification & Security Clearance: What's Required for Submarine-Grade AM Parts?**
Defence procurement for naval vessels involves strict certification (Lloyd's, DNV) and security protocols. For an AM part to be qualified for use on a submarine, what additional testing, documentation, or oversight is required compared to commercial marine applications? Who owns the CAD designs and intellectual property?

### 3. **Supply Chain Resilience for Aging Assets: Can Distributed AM Support 50+ Year Asset Lifecycles?**
Victoria-class submarines were built in the 1980s–90s. Original suppliers have consolidated, left the market, or lost technical expertise. Could a *network* of certified regional 3D printing facilities (Canada, allied nations) become the Navy's preferred backup supply chain? What would that contracting and governance model look like?

### 4. **NSERC & CFI Funding Model: Can This Approach Scale Across Defence Procurement?**
Your Dalhousie collaboration is funded by NSERC (Natural Sciences) and CFI (research infrastructure), with industry partners. If this model works for submarines, could it scale to other defence platforms (Coast Guard vessels, military trucks, aircraft)? What barriers would need to be overcome?

### 5. **Five Eyes & Allied Nation Interoperability: How Do You Handle IP and Sovereignty?**
Canada is part of NATO and Five Eyes intelligence partnerships. If AddManuChain-type platforms were used to share certified CAD designs across allied nations' defence procurement, what governance and security protocols would the Canadian Navy require? Could allies source critical spares from each other's certified networks?

---

## 2 Bonus Questions

### B1. **Hybrid Welding Research → AM Transition: What Hasn't Changed?**
Your earlier research on laser-arc hybrid welding of HSLA-65 steel (naval shipbuilding) shows deep materials expertise. As you transition from welding to 3D printing for naval applications, what materials science principles stay the same? Are there welded assemblies on Victoria-class subs that could be replaced with monolithic AM components?

### B2. **AddManuChain for Defence: Would Canada Participate?**
If we showed you a platform that allows defence departments to deposit certified CAD designs (with security controls), manage production across vetted allied facilities, maintain audit trails, and handle classified components—would DRDC/Canadian Navy consider:
  - **(a)** Contributing to platform governance/security architecture?
  - **(b)** Using platform for qualified spare parts procurement (Victoria-class, other platforms)?
  - **(c)** Neither, and if not, what's the primary concern?

---

## Interview Flow

1. **Warm-up (2 min):** Acknowledge his role at DRDC, Victoria-class initiative, and Dalhousie partnership
2. **Core discussion (40 min):** Work through Q1–Q5, focusing on naval certification, sovereign capability, and allied interoperability
3. **Strategic fit (8 min):** Bonus questions on defence-specific platform participation and governance frameworks
4. **Close (5 min):** Thank him, confirm next steps, gauge interest in future collaboration

## Notes
- **Tone:** Operational security, allied partnerships, sovereign capability—practical constraints matter
- **Leverage:** He's solving the exact problem: aging assets, supply chain fragility, need for distributed domestic capacity
- **Defence Mindset:** Sovereignty, security clearances, classified designs—explore what governance model would be acceptable
- **Listen for:** Which components are most critical, certification/testing requirements, attitudes toward allied procurement, budget constraints, timeline pressures for submarine fleet fixes
