# Interview 79: Andrew Black — Interview Questions
**GSTS - Global Spatial Technology Solutions, Director of Supply Chain Solutions**

---

## 5 Main Questions

### 1. **Port Predictability & Spare Parts: Where's the Bottleneck?**
At the Port of Montreal and other ports, the biggest friction point for ship arrivals is unpredictability in ETA. When a vessel has a critical component (engine part, navigation system) that fails or needs immediate replacement, how does your OCIANA platform help expedite the delivery of spare parts? What's the current vs. post-OCIANA lead time delta?

### 2. **Supply Chain Visibility: Who Owns the Data Chain from Vessel to Port to Shore?**
OCIANA fuses multiple data streams to predict vessel movements and port arrivals. For a distributed manufacturing and parts network (like AddManuChain), we need real-time visibility of vessel position, component demand at ports, and certification requirements. Does OCIANA currently share that level of granular data with third-party logistics or manufacturing partners, or is it kept proprietary to GSTS?

### 3. **Just-In-Time Parts for Maritime: Is 48-Hour Turnaround Realistic?**
Imagine a certified 3D printing hub in Halifax or Montreal that can produce critical marine components (pump seals, hydraulic fittings, navigation brackets) within 48 hours and have them delivered to a vessel pre-positioned at a port. What would OCIANA need to track to make that possible? What are the certification/compliance barriers?

### 4. **AI-Driven Demand Forecasting: Can You Predict Component Failures Before They Happen?**
Your platform shows ETA prediction, but does OCIANA use vessel operational data (speed, fuel consumption, age, maintenance history) to forecast which components are likely to fail or need replacement? If so, could that signal feed a manufacturing network to pre-position parts at strategic ports?

### 5. **Port Collaboration & Ecosystem: How Do You Get Ports to Trust & Share Data?**
You've built trust with Port of Montreal, Port of Halifax, and pilotage authorities. When you pitch a new ecosystem (distributed parts manufacturing), what are the key concerns these port authorities raise? How do you address cargo liability, certification, and coordination across port operators?

## 2 Bonus Questions

### B1. **Defence & Security Use Cases: How Does OCIANA Play in NATO/Five Eyes?**
The ACOA announcement mentions defence and security sector expansion into NATO and Five Eyes nations. For military/naval applications, spare parts availability is mission-critical. How would a distributed additive manufacturing network need to be different (from commercial shipping) to meet those security requirements?

### B2. **AddManuChain Platform Fit: Would GSTS Participate?**
If we showed you a platform that integrates OCIANA-fed demand signals with certified regional AM production, would GSTS consider:
  - **(a)** Licensing OCIANA data feeds to power manufacturing logistics?
  - **(b)** Becoming a reseller/integration partner for the AddManuChain ecosystem?
  - **(c)** Neither, and if not, what's missing?
