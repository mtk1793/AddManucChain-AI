# Interview 71 — Key Insights
**Brian May | Section Head, Scientific Engineering & Trials | DRDC Atlantic**

---

## TOP-LINE FINDINGS

### 1. Procurement Process Is the #1 Bottleneck — Not Technology
Brian confirmed that the biggest operational risk in defence scientific trials is **internal procurement speed**, not technology availability. DRDC literally postpones trials because they cannot procure parts and equipment fast enough through standard government channels. This validates the supply chain pain — but it also signals that the pain is *process/bureaucracy*, not just parts availability.

> *"We literally can't buy the stuff fast enough to stay on schedule."*

**Implication for AddManuChain:** Our pitch should address procurement speed, not just parts sourcing. The value proposition needs to include: faster qualified-supplier access, pre-approved digital parts libraries, and streamlined certification documentation — not just "we can print it."

---

### 2. No Qualification Standards for AM in Defence = Major Barrier
Brian (citing Cameron Monroe) confirmed that **there are no established qualification standards** for additive manufacturing in military applications. What exists is ISO guidance (best practices), not requirements. The current aviation approach — destructively test 99/100 units — is economically and operationally unworkable for defence.

> *"Introducing an uncertified part into a system that has the potential to kill people is... an entirely new avenue of investigation."*

**Implication:** This is our most important validation of the certification-first strategy. The path to defence is: Lloyd's Register / DNV certification first → then approach defence as a certified supplier. Brian implicitly endorsed this pathway without us having to suggest it.

---

### 3. New Submarine Program = Near-Term Opportunity
Brian mentioned that Canada is about to take on a **new class of submarine**, with a project announcement expected **summer 2026**. The current in-service support contract for existing submarines (Victoria class) is managed by Babcock Canada.

**Implication:** This creates a real near-term window. A new class of submarine means new parts management requirements, new supplier decisions, and potentially more openness to digital-native inventory approaches. Monitor the announcement.

**Key contacts:** Babcock Canada (current support contractor), Cameron Monroe (DRDC AM expert, Materials and Energy), Allen Williams (Perseus Design).

---

### 4. AI in Defence: Promising But Data-Starved
DRDC is actively using AI for automatic target recognition (reducing sensor operator workload). The main challenge is **insufficient training data** — defence doesn't have consumer-scale datasets.

> *"We don't have the datasets that Google has. Google has 6 billion people online every day. We don't quite have that."*

**Implication:** Our AI-for-inventory positioning is viable in defence if we can show it works with limited historical data. We should be prepared to discuss our AI's data requirements and whether it can perform well with sparse or domain-specific datasets.

---

### 5. Certification-First Strategy Was Validated Without Being Prompted
When Mahmoud mentioned the Lloyd's Register pathway, Brian responded positively and said the approach (get certified first, then approach defence) makes complete sense. He did not suggest an alternative path.

**This validates our pivot:** additive manufacturing as a certified, regulated service before targeting safety-critical industries.
