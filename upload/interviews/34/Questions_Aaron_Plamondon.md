**"As Director of Industrial & Technological Benefits at Irving Shipbuilding, you manage what I understand is a multi-billion dollar obligation to reinvest into Canadian companies. For a startup like ours — focused on digital inventory and on-demand additive manufacturing for maritime and defence spare parts — how does the ITB process work? In our meeting with Lee Vessey and the Thales team, we got a sense of the demand side — they see the spare parts challenge firsthand as a combat systems integrator. From the ITB side at Irving, is there a pathway for us to become a beneficiary, and what would we need to demonstrate?"**

- **Sheila Patterson (mentor panel)** specifically cited ITB policy as the way into defence

#### What We're Listening For

### Q3: Canadian Surface Combatant & AOPS — Spare Parts Challenges 🚢
**"Irving is building the Arctic and Offshore Patrol Ships and other related projects — these are multi-decade programs. From your vantage point, what does the spare parts supply chain look like for these vessels over a 30-year lifecycle? Are there already concerns about parts obsolescence, long lead times, or OEM dependencies — and could on-demand manufacturing be part of the solution?"**

#### Why This Matters
- AOPS & CSC are **$80+ billion programs** over decades — spare parts are a massive lifecycle cost
- Tyler Beatty's "War on Inventory" concept applies to naval vessels
- Defence ships face the **same obsolescence problem** as offshore platforms — but with added classification requirements

#### What We're Listening For
- ✅ **Lifecycle concern**: "Over 30 years, many original parts will become obsolete — OEMs go out of business"
- ✅ **Cost driver**: "Through-life support is where the real money is — the build is just the beginning"
- ✅ **AM receptivity**: "The Royal Canadian Navy is interested in onboard manufacturing capability"
- ✅ **Specific examples**: Types of parts that are already problematic (valves, brackets, seals, legacy electronics)
- ❌ **Red flag**: "The Navy has a well-established supply chain with no gaps" (no opportunity)

#### Follow-Up Probes
- "What's the estimated through-life support cost for the CSC program?"
- "Has the Navy explored onboard 3D printing for at-sea repairs?"
- "Are there specific OEM lock-in situations that create pain?"
- "How does the Navy currently handle obsolete parts — redesign? Stockpile? Cannibalize?"
- "Could a digital twin / digital inventory platform help the Navy manage spare parts across the fleet?"

### Q5: Certification & Class Society — The "Permission" Problem 🔐
**"From talking with people like Shawn Sooley and Tyler Beatty, we've learned that the #1 barrier to AM adoption isn't the technology — it's certification. Class societies like Lloyd's and DNV haven't kept pace with AM. From your unique perspective — sitting on both the MAMCE board AND running ITB at Irving — do you see a pathway to getting AM parts certified for naval and marine use? And could the defence procurement process actually ACCELERATE certification, since the government has more leverage than individual companies?"**

#### Why This Matters
- Every interview validates certification as THE barrier (Tyler's "Permission" pillar)
- Defence/government procurement may have **leverage to force class society movement**
- Shawn Sooley's "Aerospace First" strategy might have a **"Naval First" parallel**
- Aaron can speak to both the demand side (Irving/Navy) and the supply side (MAMCE)

#### What We're Listening For
- ✅ **Government leverage**: "If DND specs AM parts, class societies will have to develop standards"
- ✅ **Naval parallel to aerospace**: "The military has different risk tolerance and certification pathways"
- ✅ **Standards development**: "There are ongoing efforts — NATO, allied navies — to standardize AM qualification"
- ✅ **Specific pathway**: "Start with non-critical parts under military specs, then migrate to class society approval"
- ❌ **Red flag**: "The Navy won't accept any risk on AM parts" (no defence pathway)

#### Follow-Up Probes
- "Are NATO allies (US, UK, Australia) further ahead on naval AM certification?"
- "Could military qualification be used as evidence for civilian class society approval?"
- "Is there a specific standard emerging for marine AM parts (AWS, ASTM, ISO)?"
- "Would Irving support a pilot project — printing and qualifying a non-critical part for AOPS?"
- "Has MAMCE published any qualification data that could support certification?"
