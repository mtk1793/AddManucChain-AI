## Key Takeaways

### Supply Chain Pain Points Identified
1. **SAG Mills as Critical Bottleneck:** 40-60 foot diameter grinding mills are single point of failure
   - Bespoke, custom-built components
   - 40-60+ year asset lifecycles
   - No redundancy in supply chain

2. **Lead Time Challenge:** Components have long lead times (months to years in some cases)
   - Russian operations: 2-3 year supply chains (ice roads, Arctic logistics)
   - Standard: weeks to months for critical spares

3. **Financial Impact:** One critical spare part ($100K-$10M+) can halt million-dollar operations
   - Downtime costs vastly exceed spare parts costs
   - Supply disruption = operational shutdown

### 3D Printing / Additive Manufacturing Perspective
- **Already Considered:** Kinross has experimented with 3D printing in Russian operations
- **Use Case:** Remote sites where traditional procurement is impossible
- **Implementation:** CNC machining, specialized lathing, hot-shot parts
- **Success Factor:** Creativity in maintaining uptime when standard supply chains break down

### Investment Priority ($10M Annual Envelope)
Kobalch prioritizes **people and training** over technology investments:
- Better people running processes = better outcomes
- Technology alone insufficient without competence
- Organizational capability > Capital equipment

### Receptiveness to AddManuChain
- **Positive:** Kinross sees value in on-site 3D printing, especially for remote sites
- **Considerations:** 
  - Liability and warranty implications (parts on warranted equipment)
  - Technical capability: Can 3D-printed parts meet high-stress, heat-treated requirements?
  - Workforce complexity: Need specialized engineers vs. simple "print button" operation
  - Safety and regulatory compliance in highly mechanized, stressful industry

## Strategic Importance

**Why Kinross Matters:**
- Large-scale global operations = high-visibility proof-of-concept
- Multiple remote sites (South America, Africa, Alaska) = diverse use cases
- Already open-minded to 3D printing (prior experiments)
- Supply chain resilience = board-level priority
- High-value spares + long lead times = perfect AddManuChain use case

**Barriers:**
- Liability and warranty issues need clarity
- Technical feasibility of high-stress, heat-treated components unclear
- Workforce training and ease-of-use critical

**Next Steps:**
- Focus on technical validation: Can 3D printing match heat-treated steel requirements?
- Develop liability framework: Who carries risk for 3D-printed components?
- Prototype with small, non-critical parts first
- Build proof-of-concept at one Kinross remote site
