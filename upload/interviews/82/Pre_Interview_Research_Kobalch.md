## ORGANIZATIONAL CONTEXT: KINROSS GOLD

### Operations & Supply Chain Challenges

**Geographic Footprint:**
- South America (Chile, Brazil) — remote, high altitude, extended supply chains
- Africa — regulatory complexity, logistics challenges
- Alaska — extreme remoteness, seasonal access
- Historically Russia — extreme remoteness, 2-3 year lead times (now divested)

**Critical Equipment:**

1. **SAG Mills (Semi-Autogenous Grinding)**
   - 40-60 feet diameter
   - Bespoke, custom-built (no off-the-shelf alternatives)
   - Massive motors, agitators, structural components
   - Single point of failure = operational shutdown
   - Long lead times (months to years per component)
   - Supply from limited vendors globally

2. **Crushing & Grinding Equipment**
   - Gearless drives, motors, belts, wear plates
   - High-stress environments
   - Require heat-treating, quenching, secondary surface treatments
   - Custom maintenance approaches per site

### Financial Impact of Downtime
- **Per Day Downtime Cost:** Multiple millions (gold production = revenue)
- **Commodity Sensitivity:** At $5,000/oz, every production day = significant cash generation
- **Spare Parts Economics:** $100M+ critical spares held on-site to prevent downtime risk
- **Competitive Impact:** Competitors face same constraints; resilience = operational advantage

### Supply Chain Resilience Strategy

**Current Approach:**
1. **Inventory Holding:** Large spare parts cache on-site
2. **Supply Networks:** Relationships with main dealers, advance procurement
3. **Internal Expertise:** On-site capability to repair/modify/fabricate alternatives
4. **Proactive Planning:** 5-6 year track record of avoiding major disruptions
5. **Innovative Problem-Solving:** Creative solutions when supply chains fail (e.g., 3D printing in Russia)

**Board/CEO Priority:** HIGH (resiliency planning explicitly named as top of mind)

  - Ice road access (4-month window annually)
  - Multi-country procurement (US, Canada, China, Taiwan)
