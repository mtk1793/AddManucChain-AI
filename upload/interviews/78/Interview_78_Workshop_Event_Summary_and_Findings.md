#### Session 2: Michelin's Additive Manufacturing Journey
**Speaker:** Kerry Coolen (Michelin)

**Michelin's AM Implementation Context:**
- **Timeline:** Multi-year journey (not recent startup)
- **Infrastructure:** 8 3D printers on-site (committed investment)
- **ROI:** $500K+ annual savings documented
- **Integration:** AM is now core operational process, not experimental

**Use Case 1: Machine Safety Guards**
- **Traditional Process:** Custom guards required expensive tooling
- **AM Solution:** 3D-printed guards with rapid iteration capability
- **Results:**
  - Significant cost reduction
  - 8-16 day lead time reduction
  - Workers protected faster (health & safety benefit)
  - Operator confidence increased

**Use Case 2: Component Interface Modifications**
- **Problem:** Metal-on-metal contact causing vibration and wear
- **AM Solution:** Geometry modifications impossible with traditional casting
- **Impact:** Reduced vibration, reduced wear, improved durability, enhanced machine reliability

**Operational Insights from Michelin:**
- AM enables **floor-level problem-solving** (teams use it to address operational challenges)
- **Workforce adoption is key** (safety guards built confidence; operators see it as support tool, not replacement)
- **Practical improvements compound** (safety + reliability + efficiency feedback loop)

**Live Demonstration:**
- Two 3D printers operated on-site during workshop
- Participants observed printing process in real time
- Operational considerations highlighted: environmental controls, ventilation, printer setup, maintenance

**Key Teaching:** AM adoption requires understanding of operational realities, not just technical specs

**Strategic Value:**
Michelin's proven ROI + documented use cases = **powerful reference customer + demand anchor**

### AFTERNOON: HANDS-ON LEARNING & TOOLING

#### Session 3: Metal AM & Aerospace Certification Challenges
**Speaker:** Mark Kirby (Tronosjet)

**Focus Area:** Certifying additively manufactured components for aerospace and space applications

**Key Challenges Identified:**
1. **Rigorous qualification required:** Material testing, process validation, certification evidence
2. **Industry variation:** Different sectors have different risk tolerance and innovation appetite
3. **Application-dependent:** Risk varies by use case within same industry
4. **No universal standard:** Each application requires custom qualification approach

**Critical Insight:**
"Understanding how these industries approach certification and material qualification provided valuable perspective for manufacturers considering how additive manufacturing might apply within their own sectors."

**Implication for Non-Aerospace Sectors:**
- Aerospace has highest certification bar (rigorous but established)
- Maritime/Defence (Lloyd's, DNV, DND) = different bar
- Industrial/Mining (ISO 9001, regional compliance) = yet different bar
- **No "one size fits all" certification framework**

#### Session 5: Hands-On Part Evaluation & Design Exercise

**Exercise 1: Drone Wing Design**
- **Participants:** 13 manufacturers working in small groups
- **Task:** Evaluate 3D-printed drone wing feasibility
- **Discussion Topics (Extensive):**
  - Build orientation (positioning impact on material use, strength, support)
  - Structural considerations (load paths, stress distribution, anisotropy)
  - Support requirements and Post-processing (scaffolding cost/complexity)

**Key Insight:**
Traditional manufacturing professionals ask: "What's the design?"  
AM professionals must ask: "How should we orient it? What supports do we need? How do we post-process?"

**Environment:**
- Open dialogue with AM experts
- Questions from participants related to own operations
- Peer learning across manufacturing sectors
- Connection between design decisions and production efficiency/part performance

**Networking Outcome:**
> "Equally important was the opportunity for manufacturers to connect with one another in person. The workshop fostered strong networking and collaboration among participants."
