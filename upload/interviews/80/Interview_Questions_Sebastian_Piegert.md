# Interview 80: Sebastian Piegert — Interview Questions
**Siemens Energy, Department Lead Additive Manufacturing**

---

## 5 Main Questions

### 1. **Distributed AM for Turbines: Are There Design Governance Guardrails?**
You mentioned at the AM Forum that moving AM from strategy to shopfloor requires managing complexity. For a distributed manufacturing network (multiple printing centers globally), what are the non-negotiable design standards or certification requirements that *must* remain centralized at Siemens Energy? What can be decentralized?

### 2. **Post-Warranty Spares & Revenue Models: Does Distributed AM Threaten or Enhance Service Revenue?**
Siemens Energy has historically earned significant service revenue from spare parts for installed turbine fleets. If a certified 3D printing hub in North America could produce critical aftermarket components on-demand, how would that change Siemens Energy's service business model? Would it accelerate customer retention, or cannibalize spare parts margins?

### 3. **Supply Chain Resilience: How Critical Is Geographic Redundancy for Your Spares?**
The Winston-Salem Technology Center represents geographic expansion. For a global customer base with aging turbine fleets (40–50 year asset lifecycles), how important is *redundancy* in production capacity? Would Siemens Energy benefit from a pre-vetted network of regional production partners rather than owning all printing capacity?

### 4. **Hydrogen Transition & New Materials: How Do Legacy Components Compare?**
Your recent work on 100%-hydrogen gas turbines requires new materials and certifications. For a distributed manufacturing network, how do you manage the transition from legacy turbine spares (traditional materials) to next-gen hydrogen-ready parts? Could a platform handle both simultaneously?

### 5. **Collaboration vs. Control: Would You Open Your CAD Repository?**
You've partnered with Rolls-Royce on additive manufacturing for aerospace. For a broader ecosystem (maritime, defence, industrial), would Siemens Energy consider depositing certified CAD designs into a shared repository where vetted regional printers produce on-demand parts? What safeguards would you require?

---

## 2 Bonus Questions

### B1. **Green Manufacturing & Sustainability: Does AM Decentralization Reduce Carbon Footprint?**
One of your key messages is decarbonization (hydrogen, green energy). Does a distributed AM model—producing locally rather than shipping spares globally—align with Siemens Energy's sustainability goals? Have you modeled the carbon impact of centralized vs. decentralized production?

### B2. **AddManuChain Platform Fit: Where Would Siemens Energy Play?**
If we showed you a platform that combines design governance (yours), regional certified printing, real-time demand signals, and audit trails—would Siemens Energy consider:
  - **(a)** Depositing high-value designs as anchor tenant (licensing to network)?
  - **(b)** Operating a printing center (manufacturing-as-a-service)?
  - **(c)** Both, or neither—and if neither, what's the sticking point?

---

## Interview Flow

1. **Warm-up (2 min):** Acknowledge his Department Lead role, AM Forum presentation, and Rolls-Royce collaboration
2. **Core discussion (40 min):** Work through Q1–Q5, focusing on business model implications and distributed network design
3. **Strategic fit (8 min):** Bonus questions on platform participation and revenue model alignment
4. **Close (5 min):** Thank him, confirm next steps, gauge openness to deeper pilot discussions

---

## Notes
- **Tone:** Business model innovation, not just technology—ask about margin protection, customer retention, partner risk
- **Leverage:** He's navigating the transition from factory-owned manufacturing to ecosystem-orchestrated production—he understands the tension
- **OEM Mindset:** Siemens Energy controls quality, IP, and customer relationships; probe what would make them comfortable delegating production
- **Listen for:** Certification/qualification workflows for partners, inventory optimization strategies, customer willingness to source from third-party networks, hydrogen transition impact on supply chain
