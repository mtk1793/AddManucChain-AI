## EXPECTED OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

### Objection 1: "We already have internal systems"
**Response:**
> "That's why we're not trying to replace your systems—we integrate with them. API connections to SAP, Maximo, your maintenance logs. AddManuChain sits on top, adding AI intelligence and coordination across your multi-site network."

### Objection 2: "Certification is corporate-mandated; we can't change processes"
**Response:**
> "We're not asking SLB to change certification. We're working within Lloyd's Register standards. Our innovation is in the library—once you certify a part today, future reprints require zero re-approval. That's an SLB win, not process change."

### Objection 3: "3D printer manufacturers already offer this"
**Response:**
> "Hardware vendors sell printers & support. We're different: we're solving the meta-problem—coordinating certified printers across regions, predicting demand, managing digital inventory. Hardware is commodity; orchestration is the moat."

### Objection 4: "We need to see ROI before we invest"
**Response:**
> "Exactly. Let's start Phase 1 with 2-3 pilot parts. We'll show: cost savings, certification time reduction, and defect rate improvement. If ROI is positive (and it should be), you scale to 50+ parts."

### Objection 5: "Our operations team will resist change"
**Response:**
> "We build tools *for* operations teams, not against them. Automated print scheduling means less manual work. Predictive maintenance means fewer emergencies. Cleaner data means better decisions. It's a productivity gain, not a threat."
