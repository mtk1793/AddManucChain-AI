## 5 MAIN INTERVIEW QUESTIONS

### **QUESTION 1: PRESSURE SYSTEM FAILURES & CORRODED PARTS REPLACEMENT**

What was the cost of that failure (downtime, repair, replacement)? And critically—**could that failure have been prevented or the response accelerated with on-demand local manufacturing?**

---

### **QUESTION 2: FITNESS-FOR-SERVICE ASSESSMENTS & REMAINING LIFE PREDICTION**

> You're an expert in API 579-1 Fitness-for-Service assessments. **What's the typical workflow when you identify a component approaching end-of-life?** Do you recommend immediate replacement or schedule it into maintenance windows? How accurate are your remaining-life predictions, and what happens when a component fails ahead of predicted timeline? **If you could have replacement parts pre-manufactured and certified before failure, how would that change your risk management approach?**

---

### **QUESTION 3: MATERIAL SELECTION & CORROSION RESISTANCE IN OFFSHORE OPERATIONS**

> Your background includes material selection recommendations to prevent corrosion in offshore assets. **What materials do you specify to prevent corrosion in high-risk environments (subsea, high H₂S, high CO₂)?** Are there materials you'd prefer but can't use due to availability or cost? **If AddManuChain could manufacture corrosion-resistant components locally with certified materials, which material specs would be most valuable to have on-demand?** (e.g., duplex stainless, super-duplex, specialty alloys?)

---

### **QUESTION 4: SUPPLY CHAIN FRAGMENTATION & EMERGENCY PART SOURCING**

> Tell me about **the worst emergency you've faced where you needed a critical pressure system component immediately.** How long did sourcing take? Did you have to order by helicopter, expedite shipping, or make do with workarounds? **How much did that emergency cost Cenovus?** What would it have saved if you had pre-certified, locally-available replacements ready? And—**how many similar emergencies occur per year in your Atlantic operations?**

---

### **QUESTION 5: PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE & RISK-BASED INSPECTION INTEGRATION**

> You manage risk-based in-service inspection programs. **How do you currently schedule inspections—is it purely time-based, condition-based, or integrated with predictive models?** What data sources do you use? If you had AI-driven predictive models that could forecast component failures 3-6 months in advance with 80%+ accuracy, **how would that change your inspection strategy and parts inventory approach?** Would you print replacements proactively, or keep emergency printing capacity on standby?
