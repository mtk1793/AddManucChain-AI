## EXPERT ADVICE

### On Emergency Sourcing Reality
*"I had an example even this morning... simple nuts and bolts that I needed for pressure vessel inspection... took a week and a half to get them from the supplier."*

→ **Translation:** This is not hypothetical. Every operator faces this weekly. Emergency sourcing is built into their budgets and operations.

### On Inventory Management
*"If we carry a small stock offshore, and when the amount of inventory drops to a certain point, there's an automatic reward to refill it. But instead of refilling in the logistics of shipping... if we could print components offshore... that would be fantastic."*

→ **Translation:** This is your exact use case. Auto-triggered printing when thresholds hit. Build for this workflow.

### On Vendor Lock-in
*"We pay a lot of money for spare parts from these vendors. If we could get around that [proprietary designs] it would be a very big win."*

→ **Translation:** Long-term opportunity is huge, but start with commodity parts where IP isn't locked up.

### On Regulatory Pathway
*"The vendor can approach the DNV and say, 'We bought this new widget, how do we test it? And how do we get certification?'"*

→ **Translation:** Certification is possible but takes time. Partnership with DNV/Lloyd's = competitive advantage.
