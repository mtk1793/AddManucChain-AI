**Target:** Mike Lenart, Mill Maintenance Superintendent, Greenstone Gold Mines  
**Interview Focus:** Mining operations maintenance, spare parts supply chain, equipment downtime economics  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Date:** [To be scheduled]

## CORE INTERVIEW QUESTIONS (5 Main + 2 Bonus)

### **MAIN QUESTION 1: Current Supply Chain & Spare Parts Process**

**English:**
"Can you walk me through how you currently handle procurement of critical replacement parts for the mill? For example, if a pump seal fails or a crusher needs a replacement component, what's your process from identification to having the part in hand?"

**Farsi:**
"می‌تونید درباره فرآیند تدارکات قطعات یدکی حیاتی برای کارخانه توضیح بدید؟ مثلاً اگر یک مهر پمپ خراب شود یا یک کوبنده نیاز به تعویض دارد، فرآیند شما از تشخیص تا دستیافتن به قطعه چطوری است؟"

**Why This Matters:** Opens conversation naturally, gets him thinking about his actual workflows, reveals pain points

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- How long does this process typically take?
- Who are your main suppliers for these parts?
- What's the cost difference between standard and expedited?

---

### **MAIN QUESTION 2: Downtime Costs & Frequency**

**English:**
"When the mill faces unexpected downtime due to equipment failure, what's the typical impact? In other words, what does an hour of mill downtime actually cost Greenstone in lost production and other factors?"

**Farsi:**
"وقتی کارخانه با توقف غیرمنتظره‌ای بدلیل خرابی تجهیزات مواجه می‌شود، تأثیر آن چقدر است؟ یعنی، یک ساعت توقف کارخانه واقعاً چند هزینه‌ای برای گرینستون دارد؟"

**Why This Matters:** Quantifies the problem. If downtime costs $50K/hour, suddenly emergency parts expediting makes sense

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- How often does this happen? (Monthly? Quarterly?)
- What's typically the bottleneck? (Parts sourcing? Logistics? Other?)
- Do you ever lose access to specific suppliers seasonally?

---

### **MAIN QUESTION 3: Inventory & Strategic Stocking**

**English:**
"I noticed in your background you've worked at different remote mining sites across the world. How do you decide what spare parts to keep in stock versus ordering on-demand? What factors drive those decisions?"

**Farsi:**
"در سابقه کاری شما در مناطق دوردست معدن کاری مختلف در سراسر جهان کار کرده‌اید. چطور تصمیم می‌گیرید کدام قطعات یدکی را ذخیره کنید و چه چیزی را با اطلاع سفارش دهید؟ چه عواملی بر این تصمیمات تأثیر می‌گذارند؟"

**Why This Matters:** Reveals his strategic thinking + confirms remote site challenges

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- What's your "critical inventory" strategy?
- How much capital is tied up in spare parts inventory at Greenstone?
- Have you ever been burned by over-stocking? Under-stocking?
- Given Geraldton's remote location, how does that change your approach vs. operations closer to major cities?

---

### **MAIN QUESTION 4: Technology & Innovation Openness**

**English:**
"Thinking about emerging technologies in manufacturing - have you come across 3D printing or additive manufacturing used in mining operations? What's your reaction when you hear about the possibility of printing replacement parts on-site?"

**Farsi:**
"در مورد فناوری‌های نوظهور در تولید - آیا از چاپ سه‌بعدی در عملیات معدن کاری شنیده‌اید؟ واکنش شما چیست وقتی درباره امکان چاپ قطعات یدکی در محل می‌شنوید؟"

**Why This Matters:** Tests receptiveness to innovation, surfaces immediate objections

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- What would those parts need to meet in terms of quality/certification?
- What would concern you most about 3D-printed parts?
- If it worked and reduced downtime, who would you need to convince internally?

---

### **MAIN QUESTION 5: Pilot Opportunity & Decision Path**

**English:**
"If we could identify one specific part that causes you problems regularly - long lead times, obsolete from OEM, expensive emergency sourcing - and test on-site manufacturing of that part, would that be something worth exploring? What would need to be true for you to consider it?"

**Farsi:**
"اگر بتوانیم یک قطعه خاص را شناسایی کنیم که به طور منظم مشکل‌ایجاد می‌کند - زمان تحویل طولانی، منسوخ شده از تولیدکننده، تدارکات اضطراری مهم - و تدارک محلی این قطعه را آزمایش کنیم، آیا ارزش بررسی دارد؟ چه چیزهایی درست باید باشند تا شما آن را در نظر بگیرید؟"

**Why This Matters:** Moves from abstract to specific, tests buy-in, identifies next steps

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- What 2-3 parts would you identify as candidates?
- What timeline would you need for testing?
- Who else internally needs to approve this kind of pilot?
- If successful, how would this scale internally at Greenstone?

## INTERVIEW STRUCTURE & FLOW

### **Opening (5 min) - Build Rapport**
1. Thank him for his time
2. Acknowledge his impressive background (25+ years, global operations)
3. Explain why we're talking to him: "You operate in exactly the kind of environment where spare parts supply chain matters most"
4. Set expectations: "I'm going to ask about your current challenges with spare parts and equipment downtime, and maybe introduce a concept that could help"

### **Section 1: Current State (15 min)**
- Q1: Supply chain & parts procurement process
- Q2: Downtime costs & frequency
- Q3: Inventory strategy
- Allow him to think out loud, take notes on specific pain points

### **Section 2: Innovation & Openness (10 min)**
- Q4: 3D printing / additive manufacturing reaction
- Listen carefully to objections (quality? Cost? Certification?)
- Note what would need to be true for him to consider it

### **Section 3: Pilot & Decision (15 min)**
- Q5: Would he explore a pilot?
- Bonus Q1: Obsolescence experience
- Bonus Q2: Remote site economics
- Capture: Specific part candidates, timeline, internal stakeholders

### **Closing (5 min)**
- Summarize key insights
- Confirm next steps: "Can I get back to you with a proposal? Who else should I talk to?"
- Thank him, leave the door open

## WHAT TO LISTEN FOR

### **Pain Points to Capture**
- [ ] Specific part names that cause problems
- [ ] Lead times (how long to source?)
- [ ] Costs (emergency sourcing vs. normal?)
- [ ] Frequency (how often does this happen?)
- [ ] Remote location impact (is distance a factor?)

### **Objections to Note**
- [ ] Quality concerns (will 3D-printed parts meet specs?)
- [ ] Certification (does the part need to be certified? By whom?)
- [ ] Capital cost (would there be upfront investment?)
- [ ] Skill requirements (would his team need training?)

### **Buy-In Signals to Watch For**
- [ ] He spontaneously mentions a specific part that's problematic
- [ ] He asks questions about how it would work ("How long to print?" "What's the cost per part?")
- [ ] He identifies internal stakeholders ("I'd need to talk to our Operations Manager")
- [ ] He expresses real interest ("That could actually work for us")

## INTERVIEW OUTCOME SCENARIOS

### **Scenario A: High Interest (Best Case)**
**Signs:** He identifies specific parts, asks technical questions, sees potential pilot  
**Output:** Agreement to follow-up with procurement/ops team, identified 1-2 parts to test  
**Next Step:** Formal pilot proposal to Greenstone

### **Scenario B: Moderate Interest (Good Case)**
**Signs:** He acknowledges the problem but skeptical of solution, asks about quality/cost  
**Output:** Understanding of his pain points, clarity on what would need to be true for adoption  
**Next Step:** Deeper research on his specific concerns, circle back with solutions

### **Scenario C: Low Interest (Learning Case)**
**Signs:** He listens politely but sees no application, maintains status quo  
**Output:** Important learning about mining ops constraints, reasons to reject technology  
**Next Step:** Pivot to other mining operations with different pain points

## KEY REMINDERS

1. **Listen more than you talk** — You're here to understand his world, not sell
2. **Specific > General** — Don't ask "Do you have supply chain problems?" Ask "How long does it take to get a mill seal?"
3. **Numbers matter** — Get specific: costs, timelines, frequencies
4. **He's seen it all** — 25+ years means he's skeptical. Earn credibility through genuine curiosity
5. **Remote experience is gold** — His Suriname/Dominican Republic/Kyrgyzstan background is proof of concept for why on-site manufacturing matters
