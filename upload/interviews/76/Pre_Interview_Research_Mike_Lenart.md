- **Remote location:** Geraldton, Ontario is ~2 hours from major supply centers
- **What Mike probably does:** Maintains critical inventory, expedites sourcing, manages supplier relationships

**AddManuChain Value Prop:** On-demand spare parts manufacturing reduces stockpiling costs + eliminates wait times

### **2. EQUIPMENT DOWNTIME COSTS**
- **Why it matters:** Every hour of downtime = lost ore processing = lost revenue
- **Mining economics:** High fixed costs (labor, utilities, capital) fixed regardless of production
- **Current approach:** Emergency sourcing, expedited shipping (expensive), sometimes equipment substitution

**AddManuChain Value Prop:** Local printing of replacement parts (valves, seals, connectors) in hours vs. days/weeks

### **3. REMOTE LOCATION CONSTRAINTS**
- **Why it matters:** Geraldton is ~250km from Toronto, ~400km from Montreal
- **Supplier access:** Limited local suppliers for specialized mining equipment
- **Transportation:** Expensive emergency delivery from major centers
- **Weather:** Canadian winters can delay shipping

**AddManuChain Value Prop:** On-site capability eliminates transportation delays + logistics costs

### **4. CRITICAL INVENTORY MANAGEMENT**
- **Why it matters:** Must balance inventory carrying costs vs. stockout risk
- **Current solution:** Maintain "insurance" spare parts (expensive)
- **Problem:** High-value equipment parts sitting unused until needed, but when needed = emergency

**AddManuChain Value Prop:** Just-in-time manufacturing replaces inventory carrying costs

### **5. EQUIPMENT OBSOLESCENCE**
- **Why it matters:** As mines age, replacement parts become harder to source
- **Current challenge:** OEM discontinues parts → must find third-party alternatives (risky, expensive)
- **Mike's experience:** Worked across multiple mining operations with aging equipment

**AddManuChain Value Prop:** Digital design archive + manufacturing platform = parts available forever
