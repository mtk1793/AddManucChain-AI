## RELEVANCE TO LAB2MARKET

Niraj's expertise is particularly valuable to Lab2Market across five dimensions:

1. **IP as a Trust Signal:** Lab2Market's core value proposition relies partly on verified certifications and credentials that shift buyer confidence. Niraj's IP commercialization work is essentially about building and signalling trustworthiness to buyers — directly relevant to whether "verified" badges work.

2. **Springboard as a Distribution Channel:** If Lab2Market wants to reach Atlantic Canadian advanced manufacturing facilities (including those commercializing research-lab processes), Springboard Atlantic's 19-institution network is a direct channel. Niraj can confirm or challenge whether that pathway is viable.

3. **Research-Lab-to-Industry Supplier Gap:** Many advanced manufacturing capabilities (including 3D printing and additive manufacturing) exist inside university labs and research institutions that are underused by industrial buyers. Niraj sits precisely at the interface of this gap — do industrial buyers engage with research-lab capacity, and what would change that?

4. **Defence Industrial Strategy Implications:** Canada's new "Build-Partner-Buy" framework explicitly prioritizes domestic suppliers and IP ownership for sovereign capability. This creates tailwinds for a platform like Lab2Market that surfaces certified Canadian advanced manufacturing suppliers. Niraj can assess how real this policy shift is in practice.

5. **IP Barriers in the Supply Chain:** When a small 3D printing facility tries to offer services to an industrial buyer, IP ownership questions can stall deals. Who owns the IP in a custom-manufactured component? This is a friction point Niraj can speak to directly.

## KEY INTERVIEW THEMES

1. **IP Trust Signals:** Does IP ownership or IP verification influence how industrial buyers evaluate a new supplier? Is it a deal-maker or deal-breaker at the SME level?

2. **Research-to-Industry Pathway:** How well-connected are Atlantic Canadian university research labs and advanced manufacturing facilities to actual industrial buyers? Where is the gap?

3. **ElevateIP and Certification:** How does the ElevateIP program work in practice for a small advanced manufacturing facility — what does it take for them to go from "idea" to "commercially ready certified supplier"?

4. **Defence Industrial Strategy:** How concrete is the "Build-Partner-Buy" push in terms of creating real procurement opportunities for domestic advanced manufacturing suppliers?

5. **Springboard as Channel:** Can Springboard Atlantic's network accelerate Lab2Market's go-to-market by connecting it to both the supply side (research labs with manufacturing capacity) and the demand side (industry partners looking for local certified suppliers)?

6. **IP Barriers in Procurement:** What IP-related frictions slow down supplier onboarding in industries like aerospace, defence, and energy — and what reduces them?
