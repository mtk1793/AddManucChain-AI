## RELEVANCE TO LAB2MARKET

Aurangzeb is the most directly relevant enterprise buyer-side voice interviewed to date. WESCO's position in the industrial supply chain is precisely the kind of buyer Lab2Market needs to serve:

1. **Supplier Discovery at Scale:** WESCO sources specialized components for industrial clients. When a client needs a custom or specialty-manufactured part (potentially 3D printed or precision-fabricated), WESCO needs to find a qualified supplier. How that process works today — and where it fails — is central to Lab2Market's problem thesis.

2. **Supplier Qualification Time and Cost:** The time and cost of qualifying a new supplier at an organization like WESCO is significant. If Lab2Market can reduce that friction through verified, pre-certified supplier profiles, it addresses a real operational pain point.

3. **Tariff and Supply Chain Resilience:** In 2026, with US tariffs and supply chain reshoring pressure, WESCO Canada is likely under internal and external pressure to demonstrate Canadian content sourcing. Lab2Market's local supplier network has direct relevance to this mandate.

4. **Trust and Credibility Requirements:** WESCO will have specific criteria for adding a supplier — certifications, insurance, quality systems, track record, references. Understanding exactly what those criteria are defines what Lab2Market's "verified" badge needs to cover to be genuinely useful to a WESCO-level buyer.

5. **Procurement Manager Decision-Making:** Aurangzeb can describe the human decision process — how procurement managers at WESCO are incentivized, what makes them cautious about new suppliers, and what would make them open to a digital discovery platform.

1. **Supplier Qualification Process:** How long does it take, what does it require, and where does it fail for specialized or new local suppliers?
