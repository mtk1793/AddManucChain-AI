    55→### ✅ **CERTIFICATION = PRIMARY BOTTLENECK (NOT TECHNICAL CAPABILITY)**
    56→
    57→**Status:** **HIGHEST BARRIER VALIDATED** (More than business case!)
    58→
    59→**Dr. Nasiri's Framework:**
    60→
    61→**Why Certification is Hard:**
    62→
    63→1. **"AM developed so fast that authorities couldn't catch up"** ← Best quote
    64→2. **Regulatory bodies (DNV, ASTM, Lloyd's Register) developing standards but:**
    65→   - "All recommendations are too general"
    66→   - "Not specifically talking about certain types of components"
    67→
    68→**What Must Be Certified:**
    69→
    70→| Item | Complexity | Cost Impact |
    71→|------|------------|-------------|
    72→| **The Part** | Materials testing, structural validation | High |
    73→| **The Operator** | Personnel qualification | Medium |
    74→| **The Powder/Feedstock** | Batch consistency, traceability | High |
    75→| **Processing Variables** | Machine calibration, temperature, speed | High |
    76→| **The Labs** | Third-party validation facility accreditation | Very High |
    77→
    78→**Dr. Nasiri's Quote:**
    79→> "The whole process is **costly and time demanding**. This application is for the part where the application is structural."
    80→
    81→**Current Process:**
    82→1. AM facility produces part
    83→2. Contact registry (DNV, Lloyd's, ASTM)
    84→3. Hire them as subcontractor
    85→4. They provide "pathway" (not standardized)
    86→5. Step-by-step validation using accredited labs
    87→6. Part-by-part certification (no template reuse!)
    88→
    89→**Why Oil & Gas Avoids AM:**
    90→> "Oil and gas companies don't do mass production. One or 5 parts and they don't need to enter the certification process so they prefer to be more on the conventional side. **Even if AM could give them lots of opportunities, that certification alone causes lots of issues** so they don't like that process."
    91→
    92→**Hypothesis NH.7 Update:** ❌ Was "Certification is A barrier" → ✅ **"Certification is THE barrier"**

    96→### ✅ **STRUCTURAL vs NON-STRUCTURAL FRAMEWORK (CRITICAL PIVOT)**
    97→
    98→**Status:** **ADOPTION PATHWAY VALIDATED**
    99→
   100→**Dr. Nasiri's Advice:**
   101→> "**Focusing too much of your time on AM of structural parts could be challenging and time consuming. It would be better to focus on non-structural and less risky.** The part should be in the phase of **non-critical and non-simple manufacturing** section to be more acceptable."
   102→
   103→**Definitions:**
   104→
   105→**Structural Part:**
   106→- Under loading and movement
   107→- Higher risk of replacement
   108→- Under a lot of stress
   109→- Failure = operational chaos
   110→- **Certification required** (costly, time-demanding)
   111→
   112→**Non-Structural Part:**
   113→- Not load-bearing
   114→- Failure doesn't stop operations
   115→- Easier to certify OR no certification needed
   116→- **This is your entry point**
   117→
   118→**Sweet Spot:** "Non-critical AND non-simple manufacturing"
   119→- Complex enough that AM adds value (not just machining a block)
   120→- Non-critical enough that certification is manageable
   121→
   122→**Examples from Other Interviews:**
